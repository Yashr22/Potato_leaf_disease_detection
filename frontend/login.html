Crop diseases and pest infestations are major threats to agricultural productivity, and early detection through leaf image analysis is crucial for timely intervention. Common diseases such as powdery mildew, leaf blight, rust, and bacterial spots often appear as discolored patches, spots, or powdery coatings on leaves, severely affecting the plant's photosynthesis and overall health. These symptoms can be easily captured and analyzed using image-based models. Additionally, pests like aphids, caterpillars, leaf miners, and whiteflies leave distinct patterns of damage, including holes, trails, and leaf curling, which can also be identified through visual data. Our model is designed to detect and classify these symptoms accurately, helping farmers take timely action. To prevent such issues, it is recommended to use disease-free seeds, maintain adequate plant spacing for airflow, rotate crops regularly, use organic treatments, and remove infected parts promptly. These practices not only enhance disease resistance but also support sustainable farming by reducing the need for excessive chemical inputs.
